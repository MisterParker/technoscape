Technoscape18



Need

Since water is one of the most important part of our lives. It's purification before consumption is necessary in today's world especially with our ever dwindling supply.


Demand

The necessity of the hour has shifted it's focus towards innovative methods to help in the advancement of water purification technologies.



Solution

In pursuit of this, the Department of Chemical Engineering, VIT in association with InDA(SZ) and YNU is convening Technoscape 18, an international conference on Sustainable water resources - Innovations and Impacts.

Objective

The conference aims to provide an interactive knowledge sharing platform for experts, researchers, engineers, scientists and industrialists working in the field of water purification around the globe.


About Us

VIT University


Founded in 1984, as Vellore Engineering College, the Institute was upgraded as a University in recognition of its academic excellence in 2001. The institution was founded by the North Arcot Educational Trust under the chairmanship of the present Chancellor Dr. G. Viswanathan. The university has a student strength of 24,000 with more than 1400 faculty members. It has 19 Undergraduate and 34 Postgraduate programmes as well as research programmes including Ph.D.

Department of Chemical Engineering


Established in 1994, The Department of Chemical Engineering,VIT University is one of the oldest departments. The faculties of the deparment bring to the university the experiences they have garnered from institutes such as Indian Institute of Technology, the Indian Institute of Science and other premier institutes with expertise in various areas of specialization. They are actively involved in sponsored research, consultancy work and collaborative R&D projects with various regional and national industries and government agencies. Apart from this, the department also liaises with CO2 Research facility which caters to green technological projects likeÂ better utilization of carbon dioxide to meet the global need for renewable fuels using agro materials as feed stock.

About Vellore


Vellore, also called Fort City, is located on the banks of the Palar River in the north eastern part of Tamil Nadu and boasts a rich history and cultural heritage. The city is home to Christian Medical College and VIT University, both of which are among the top ten Institutions in their respective fields. It is also a major centre for medical tourism in India. The Vellore region is the top exporter of leather goods in the country and also home to several manufacturing and automobile companies. The city offers great tourist spots such as Vellore Fort, Government Museum, Science Park, and Religious destinations such as Jalakandeshwar Temple and Srilakshmi Golden Temple. Other places of interest near Vellore include hill stations like Yercaud and Yelagiri as well as Kanchipuram and Mahabalipuram, famed for their architectural magnificence.

About Yokohama National University


Yokohama National University (YNU) was established in 1949. Today, the university comprises 5 undergraduate Colleges and 5 Graduate Schools. It has developed into a university with more than 1,000 staff and faculty and 10,000 students including nearly 1,000 international students. YNU established 6 international branches to strengthen international exchange, and from an international joint research base. YNU also established 7 international liaison officers for education, research and alumni association activities abroad.

About InDA (SZ)


The Indian Desalination Association (South Zone) InDA(SZ) was formed by Indian Desalination Association (InDA) to serve the specific needs of southern region of peninsular India in 2013. Besides following the goals of InDA, the InDA(SZ) would specifically strive to build awareness about the value of water and its management through an integrated manner encompassing all the facets of water, right from sourcing to ultimate disposal. It would to strive to bring together the academics and the industries to work in synergism enabling innovations to provide clean drinking water as well as distribution methods and harvesting. The major activities would include publication of periodic bulletins and boklets covering articles on various water related aspects, holding awareness lectures and facilitating the entrepreneurs towards development of indigenous technologies for water purification.
